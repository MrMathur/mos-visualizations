1 Qualitative Dataset, Visualized 5 ways

Below are five different interactive visualizations of one qualitative dataset, a 2-minute video from a bilingual elementary classroom. The video shows the teaching of a key idea in the physics of matter—that matter occupies space—in a kindergarten-first grade classroom. In the video, a teacher is seated with her students around her on the floor of a classroom and conducts a brief experiment involving a jar and marbles to illustrate how matter occupies space. The teacher also facilitates a discussion about the experiment with the class. The visualizations below explore the value of interactive transcripts for qualitative research and traditions of interaction analysis. These visualizations use a medium called D3.js and for each visualization, we unpack and analyze the a) specific representation and interaction techniques, b) encoding decisions and c) insights generated by each visualization

  • Word Count Lollipop Chart
  • Dialogue Bubble Chart
  • Word Count Unit visualization
  • Volume Scatter Plot
  • Position and Gaze diagram
