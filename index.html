Interactive Transcription Techniques for Interaction Analysis

Hi there! Below are five different interactive visualizations of one qualitative dataset, a 2-minute video from a bilingual elementary classroom shown above. This video shows the teaching of a key idea in the physics of matter—that matter occupies space—in a kindergarten-first grade classroom. In the video, a teacher is seated with her students around her on the floor of a classroom and conducts a brief experiment and discussion involving a jar and marbles to illustrate how matter occupies space. The visualizations below explore the stengths and weaknesses of current interactive visualization techniques to amplify transcription in qualitative research for interaction analysis. These visualizations are built using a programming language called D3.js and for each visualization, we describe the 1) specific representation and interaction techniques, 2) encoding decisions and 3) insights generated

  • Word Count Lollipop Chart
  • Dialogue Bubble Chart
  • Position and Gaze diagram
  • Word Count Unit visualization
  • Volume Scatter Plot

You can also see these visualizations in action together as an integrated dashboard
