1 Qualitative Dataset, Visualized 5 ways

This webpage visualizes one qualitative dataset, a video from a bilingual elementary classroom, in 5 different ways in order to further illustrate the value of visualization to amplify current transcription practices in qualitative research.

This 2-minute video of classroom interaction shows the teaching of a key idea in the physics of matter—that matter occupies space—in a kindergarten-first grade classroom. Thus, it is a highly unstructured, minimally edited, or qualitative dataset. In the video, a teacher is seated with her students around her on the floor of a classroom and conducts a brief experiment involving a jar and marbles to illustrate how matter occupies space. The teacher also facilitates a discussion about the experiment with the class.

The following links show five different interactive visualizations that dynamically visualize this sequence of interaction in five different ways. These visualizations use a medium called D3.js to transcribe and dynamically visualize the video from this sequence of interaction. We unpack and analyze each visualization by describing the a) specific representation and interaction techniques, b) encoding decisions and c) insights generated in each visualization.

  • Word Count Lollipop Chart
  • Dialogue Bubble Chart
  • Word Count Unit visualization
  • Volume Scatter Plot
  • Position and Gaze diagram
